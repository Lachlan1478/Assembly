======================================================================
PHASE SUMMARIES
======================================================================

Phase: ETHICAL_ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In this meeting phase, participants engaged in a rigorous analysis of
the Trolley Problem through the lens of both utilitarian and
deontological ethical frameworks. There was a strong emphasis on
identifying key aspects for examination, highlighting the importance
of moral duties and consequences in ethical decision-making. While no
changes to foundational beliefs were made, the discussion underscored
the need to carefully consider moral obligations versus outcomes in
ethical assessments.

Phase: ETHICAL_INTEGRATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In this meeting phase, participants identified common ground between
utilitarian and deontological perspectives on the Trolley Problem,
emphasizing the importance of considering outcomes in ethical
decision-making while upholding individual rights. Both the Pragmatic
Ethicist and the Empathetic Listener acknowledged that maximizing
overall well-being is crucial but must be balanced with the unwavering
respect for individual rights, suggesting a framework for decisions
that seeks to achieve maximum well-being without sacrificing ethical
principles.
