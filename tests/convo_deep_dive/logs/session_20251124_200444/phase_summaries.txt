======================================================================
PHASE SUMMARIES
======================================================================

Phase: ETHICAL_ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In this meeting phase, participants engaged in a detailed analysis of
the Trolley Problem, utilizing both utilitarian and deontological
ethical perspectives. While the Utilitarian Ethicist advocated for the
consideration of outcomes in ethical decision-making, the
Deontological Philosopher emphasized the importance of moral duties
and the inherent value of individuals, ultimately affirming their
belief that ends do not justify means. The discussion highlighted the
complexities and limitations of each ethical framework without
reaching a consensus on the dilemma.

Phase: ETHICAL_INTEGRATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
In this phase, participants engaged in an exploration of the Trolley
Problem, highlighting the complexity of ethical decision-making as it
relates to utilitarian and deontological perspectives. A key outcome
was the identification of the conflicting nature of these ethical
frameworks, emphasizing the need for a nuanced understanding of their
implications in moral dilemmas. The conversation set the stage for
potential strategies to reconcile these diverging ethical approaches
in future discussions.
